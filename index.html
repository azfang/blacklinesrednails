● 1. LANGUAGE & IMAGE

There is something unmistakably beautiful to be found in conversations between the intangible. Ink bleeding through pages, leaving accidental imprints on the other end. Spoken words being picked up and tossed away by the wind.

In unexpected dialogues between language and image, typography concretizes and holds the fleeting concepts of the image. The power in stylized words in influencing photography is manifold. Meaning can be added, subtracted, with too much or too little pared back and stripped away: “Language and image, each trespassing in the other’s house, secrete disquieting disjunctions, conundrums, circularities. We are accustomed to the poetic strategy, within language, of bracketing a noun within the genus of yet another noun [...] Translation of that strategy into the economy of images yields artifacts…. savagely grotesque, arch, silly.”1 These words, written by Hollis Frampton, speak of the attribution of meaning and its impact—possibly damaging, potentially misappropriating—on photography.
